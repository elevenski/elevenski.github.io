Hello, I’m Eleven — a self-taught web developer who cares about simplicity, clarity, and making things that just work.

I got into software development when I was around 10 years old, and I’ve been trying to improve myself since 2015–2016. My first project was a somewhat silly attempt at building a coal-powered Discord bot—but that was the spark that pulled me into coding. For years, I worked on Discord bots, experimenting with APIs, commands, and small features that helped me understand how things connect and function.

Later on, my focus shifted to website development and UI design. I’ve always preferred things that are simple, clean, and easy to use. That reflects in how I build stuff—I usually avoid unnecessary complexity, write in an understandable way, and keep everything free and accessible. I believe software should make life easier, not harder.

In the past couple of years, I haven’t released any new projects or done much coding at all. I’ve stepped away from development a bit, and my interest has faded compared to before. Still, I’ve kept updating and refining my personal website. It’s been a small way to stay connected with the creative and technical side of things, even if I'm not actively building.

Right now, I’m not sure where I stand with software—but I still value the process of learning, creating, and making things that are clear and useful. Whether I return to development fully or not, that mindset will probably stick with me.
